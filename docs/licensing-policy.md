# Licensing Policy

## Status
QSO-SEEKER is **source-available**, not OSI-approved open source. The project permits study, academic research, noncommercial modification, replication, and community contribution, while restricting commercial exploitation.

The Open Source Definition requires free redistribution and does not permit a license to prohibit selling the software. Because this project intentionally restricts resale and commercial incorporation, it must not be described as open source.

## Core rule
No person or organization may package, resell, sublicense, host for payment, incorporate into a commercial product, use internally for commercial advantage, or use the project to train a proprietary or monetized AI system without prior written authorization.

## Ecosystem-commercial exception
Revenue-generating work may occur within or for the QSO ecosystem only under a separate written agreement that returns a measurable benefit to the ecosystem. The agreement may require revenue sharing, equity, fees, infrastructure, data contributions, upstream code, public research, or another defined benefit.

Simply publishing a fork, submitting a contribution, or describing a product as “compatible with QSO” does not create commercial permission.

## Academic use
Researchers may clone the repositories, collect lawful data, replicate experiments, publish findings, submit negative results, and return sanitized academic contribution bundles. Academic attribution, provenance, source licensing, privacy, and research-ethics duties remain mandatory.

A university, public laboratory, charity, or independent researcher may receive funding and still qualify for research use when the software itself is not sold, licensed, embedded in a commercial service, or used under a commercial deployment arrangement.

## Corporate evaluation
A commercial entity may inspect the public source and conduct a limited internal evaluation solely to decide whether to request a commercial ecosystem agreement. It may not use the software in production, product development, customer work, model training, or revenue-generating operations without authorization.

## Third-party material
The QSO Ecosystem Source License does not override licenses or terms governing third-party datasets, APIs, publications, models, or contributed materials. Every collection and contribution must preserve those separate rights and restrictions.

## Legal review
The current license is marked as a draft for legal review. Before a tagged production release, counsel should review enforceability, governing law, contributor assent, patent language, commercial-accounting obligations, and compatibility with intended academic data licenses.
